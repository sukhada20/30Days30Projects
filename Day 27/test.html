Protection Guides

Learn how to protect yourself from solar and UV radiation risks.

Solar Flares Protection

Solar flares can disrupt communications and power grids. Here's how to prepare:

  • Keep backup power sources charged
  • Download offline maps and important information
  • Have alternative communication methods ready
  • Follow alerts from space weather agencies

Geomagnetic Storms Protection

Geomagnetic storms can affect power grids and satellites:

  • Unplug sensitive electronic equipment during severe storms
  • Have backup plans for GPS-dependent activities
  • Be prepared for potential power outages
  • Keep emergency supplies on hand

Solar Radiation Protection

Solar radiation can be harmful, especially for travelers:

  • Avoid air travel during severe solar radiation events
  • Pregnant women and those with health concerns should consult doctors
  • Astronauts and aviation personnel follow special protocols
  • Monitor space weather forecasts before long flights

UV Protection Guide

Protect yourself from harmful UV radiation:

  • Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen every 2 hours
  • Wear protective clothing, hats, and sunglasses
  • Seek shade between 10 AM and 4 PM
  • Be extra cautious near reflective surfaces (water, sand, snow)

Skin Health Guide

Maintain healthy skin despite UV exposure:

  • Perform regular skin self-examinations
  • Know your skin type and its sensitivity
  • Use moisturizers with SPF daily
  • See a dermatologist annually for skin checks

Children Protection Guide

Special considerations for protecting children:

  • Use baby-safe sunscreen for children over 6 months
  • Keep infants under 6 months out of direct sunlight
  • Dress children in UV-protective clothing
  • Schedule outdoor activities in early morning or late afternoon
